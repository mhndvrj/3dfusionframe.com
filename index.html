The Challenge

Why Existing 3D Technology Fails

Every 3D display technology in the market today shares the same fundamental limitations — limitations that have prevented true mass adoption for decades.

🕶️

Glasses Always Required

Active or passive glasses are uncomfortable, expensive to maintain, and impractical in professional environments like operating theatres or cockpits.

👁️

Excludes Monocular Viewers

All stereoscopic systems require two functional eyes. Over 45 million people worldwide with monocular vision are entirely excluded from 3D experiences.

🧠

Brain Does All the Work

Fusion happens in the viewer's visual cortex — not the display. This causes eye strain, headaches, and fatigue with prolonged 3D viewing.

📐

Restricted Viewing Angles

Glasses-free autostereoscopic displays demand precise head positioning within a narrow "sweet spot" — impractical for real-world use.

💰

High Infrastructure Cost

Active 3D systems require expensive electronics, battery-powered glasses, and complex synchronisation — all adding cost and failure points.

🔬

No In-Display Fusion

No existing technology performs optical image fusion within the display medium itself. Until now.

The Invention

How the Fusion Frame Works

The 3D Optical Fusion Frame performs optical image fusion within the frame medium itself — before the image reaches the viewer's eyes. This is a fundamentally different approach from all existing 3D technologies.

01

Two Source Images Introduced

Two offset images are introduced into the frame's optical system — similar in principle to stereoscopic source material.

02

Optical Fusion Inside the Frame

The frame's internal optical medium physically merges the two images. Fusion is a physical process, not a neurological one.

03

Single Depth-Encoded Output

The frame outputs a single, already-fused image containing full 3D depth information — no brain processing required.

04

Any Viewer Perceives 3D

Because fusion is complete at the frame, even a person with a single functional eye perceives the full 3D depth effect.
